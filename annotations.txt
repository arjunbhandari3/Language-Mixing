Sample Annotations  
Example 1: Mixed script Nepali–English (Romanized)  
“Aaja office ma meeting cha, tara mobile charge bhayena.” 
(Today there’s a meeting in the office, but my mobile isn’t charged.) 
Token Language Borrowing Explanation 
Aaja NE NATIVE Nepali word (today) 
office EN BORROWED Established loanword 
ma NE NATIVE Nepali postposition (in) 
meeting EN BORROWED Integrated into Nepali 
cha NE NATIVE Nepali copula (is) 
tara NE NATIVE Nepali conjunction (but) 
mobile EN BORROWED Common loanword 
charge EN BORROWED English “charge” 
bhayena NE NATIVE Nepali negation 
 
Example 2: Code-switched vs. borrowed distinction  
“Ma computer kinna janu parcha because processor purano bhaisakyo.” 
(I have to go buy a computer because the processor has become old.) 
Token Language Borrowing Explanation 
computer EN BORROWED Established loanword 
because EN CODE-MIXED Temporary insertion 
processor EN BORROWED Technical term 
purano NE NATIVE Nepali “old”
